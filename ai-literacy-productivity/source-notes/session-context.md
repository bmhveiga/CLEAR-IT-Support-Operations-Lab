**# Session Context – AI Literacy for Productivity**



**## Training Module**



**This lab is based on \*\*Session 1 – AI Literacy for Productivity\*\* from the \*\*M5 Remote Work and Freelance Toolkit\*\* module.**



**The session focused on the strategic use of Artificial Intelligence in professional digital contexts, especially for productivity, remote work, freelance workflows, client communication, and operational organization.**



**## Main Theme**



**The main theme of the session was using AI as a professional productivity tool.**



**The focus was not only on generating text with AI, but on using AI through a structured process:**



**1. Understand the problem.**

**2. Define the objective.**

**3. Create a structured prompt.**

**4. Generate an initial output.**

**5. Review the result critically.**

**6. Improve the output through iterative prompting.**

**7. Validate the final version before using it professionally.**



**## Case Study**



**The exercise was based on a simulated company called \*\*Cabo Digital Travel\*\*.**



**Cabo Digital Travel is a tourism and local experiences company that grew through digital promotion. As demand increased, the company started facing operational challenges related to remote work, client communication, task management, and process organization.**



**## Main Operational Challenges**



**The company had several challenges:**



**\* delayed responses to clients;**

**\* inconsistent digital communication;**

**\* difficulty organizing tasks and priorities;**

**\* repetitive administrative tasks;**

**\* unstructured workflows;**

**\* difficulty scaling content production;**

**\* lack of standardized operational documentation;**

**\* inefficient use of digital tools and AI.**



**## Company Goals**



**The company wanted support in:**



**\* improving digital workflows;**

**\* increasing operational productivity;**

**\* improving professional communication with clients;**

**\* organizing tasks and documentation;**

**\* using AI strategically;**

**\* reducing time spent on repetitive work;**

**\* creating simple operational solutions.**



**## Tools Mentioned in the Scenario**



**The company used or wanted to improve the use of:**



**\* Google Workspace;**

**\* Trello;**

**\* Slack;**

**\* Google Meet;**

**\* generative AI tools;**

**\* collaborative online documents.**



**## My Role in the Lab**



**In this lab, I assumed the role of a remote freelancer / digital consultant helping the company improve its productivity and communication workflows.**



**My task was to analyze the case, create AI prompts, generate professional outputs, improve those outputs, and structure a final workflow that could help the company manage client requests more efficiently.**



**## Selected Outputs**



**For this lab, I selected two practical outputs:**



**1. A professional client response email.**

**2. An operational checklist for managing client requests.**



**These outputs were selected because they directly address the company’s main problems: delayed client responses, inconsistent communication, and unstructured workflows.**



**## Professional Relevance**



**This lab is relevant to IT Support and support operations because it shows how structured communication, ticket-style thinking, documentation, workflow organization, and AI-assisted productivity can improve service delivery.**



**Although the scenario is based on a tourism company, the same method can be applied to IT Support:**



**\* receiving a user request;**

**\* gathering missing information;**

**\* documenting the issue;**

**\* assigning priority;**

**\* using templates;**

**\* validating responses;**

**\* following up;**

**\* closing and documenting the case.**



**## Key Principle**



**AI can support professional productivity, but the professional remains responsible for context, quality, validation, privacy, and final decision-making.**
