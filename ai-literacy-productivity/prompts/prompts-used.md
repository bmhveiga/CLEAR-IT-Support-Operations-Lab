**# Prompts Used – AI Literacy for Productivity**



**## Purpose**



**This file documents the prompts used during the AI-assisted productivity exercise.**



**The goal was to demonstrate how AI outputs improve when prompts include clear context, role, objective, audience, format, tone, constraints, and quality criteria.**



**The exercise used iterative prompting: first creating a basic prompt, reviewing the output, identifying limitations, and then improving the prompt to generate a more professional result.**



**---**



**# Output 1 – Professional Client Response Email**



**## Initial Prompt**



**```text**

**Create an email to respond to a client interested in Cabo Digital Travel's services.**

**```**



**## Why This Prompt Was Limited**



**This prompt was too generic.**



**It did not include:**



**\* the role the AI should assume;**

**\* the company context;**

**\* the type of client;**

**\* the purpose of the email;**

**\* the tone expected;**

**\* what information should be requested from the client;**

**\* any professional constraints.**



**Because of that, the first output was polite but basic. It did not help the company collect enough information to prepare a proper response or proposal.**



**## Improved Prompt**



**```text**

**Assume the role of a communication assistant for Cabo Digital Travel, a tourism and local experiences company in Cape Verde.**



**Create a professional email to respond to an international client interested in local experiences and customized tours.**



**The email should be cordial, clear, and objective. It should thank the client for contacting the company, briefly explain the service, ask for important information needed to prepare a proposal, and suggest either an online meeting or continuing the conversation by email.**



**Do not promise availability before confirmation.**



**Write in simple professional English.**

**```**



**## Why This Prompt Was Better**



**The improved prompt gave the AI more context and clearer instructions.**



**It included:**



**\* a professional role;**

**\* company background;**

**\* target audience;**

**\* expected tone;**

**\* specific communication goals;**

**\* operational constraints;**

**\* final language requirement.**



**This helped generate an email that was more practical, professional, and useful for a remote-work business context.**

**# Output 2 – Operational Checklist for Client Requests**



**## Initial Prompt**



**```text**

**Create a checklist for Cabo Digital Travel to organize client requests.**

**```**



**## Why This Prompt Was Limited**



**This prompt was also too simple.**



**It did not explain:**



**\* the company's remote-work context;**

**\* the tools used by the company;**

**\* the operational challenges;**

**\* where AI should help;**

**\* where human validation is necessary;**

**\* how the checklist should support a small team.**



**The first output was useful as a basic list, but it was not detailed enough to guide a real workflow.**



**## Improved Prompt**



**```text**

**Assume the role of a digital operations consultant specialized in productivity and remote work.**



**Create a simple operational checklist for Cabo Digital Travel to manage client requests in a more organized way.**



**The company is a small tourism and local experiences business that works partially remotely and uses Google Workspace, Trello, Slack, Google Meet, and generative AI tools.**



**The checklist should cover the process from receiving a client request to final follow-up.**



**Include where AI can support the process and where human validation is required.**



**Use clear, practical, and professional English.**

**```**



**## Why This Prompt Was Better**



**The improved prompt made the task more realistic and operational.**



**It included:**



**\* consultant role;**

**\* company type;**

**\* remote-work context;**

**\* tools used;**

**\* workflow scope;**

**\* AI support points;**

**\* human validation points;**

**\* clear output expectations.**



**This resulted in a checklist that was more structured, easier to apply, and more relevant to remote work operations.**



**---**



**# Prompting Strategy Used**



**The prompting strategy followed this structure:**



**## 1. Role**



**The prompt defined the role the AI should assume, such as communication assistant or digital operations consultant.**



**## 2. Context**



**The prompt explained the company situation, the business area, and the remote-work environment.**



**## 3. Objective**



**The prompt clearly stated what needed to be produced.**



**## 4. Audience**



**The prompt considered who would read or use the output: clients, internal team members, or remote workers.**



**## 5. Format**



**The prompt specified the expected format, such as email or checklist.**



**## 6. Constraints**



**The prompt included limits and cautions, such as avoiding promises of availability before confirmation.**



**## 7. Quality Criteria**



**The prompt requested clear, practical, professional, and useful outputs.**



**---**



**# Key Learning**



**The quality of AI outputs depends strongly on the quality of the prompt.**



**A basic prompt can generate a basic answer, but a structured prompt can produce a more useful professional output.**



**The most important improvement was adding context, role, objective, format, tone, and validation requirements.**



**AI is more effective when used as part of a professional workflow instead of as a simple text generator.**
