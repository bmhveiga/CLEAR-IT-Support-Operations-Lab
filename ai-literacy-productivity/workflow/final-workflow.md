**# Final Workflow – AI-Assisted Client Request Management**



**## Purpose**



**This file presents the final structured workflow developed for the Cabo Digital Travel case study.**



**The workflow shows how AI can support a small remote team in managing client requests more efficiently, while keeping human validation, professional judgment, and data protection at the center of the process.**



**---**



**# Workflow Overview**



**The proposed workflow follows eight main stages:**



**1. Receive the client request.**

**2. Analyze and summarize the request.**

**3. Collect missing information.**

**4. Organize the request internally.**

**5. Prepare the initial response or proposal.**

**6. Send the response to the client.**

**7. Follow up.**

**8. Close and document the request.**



**---**



**# Stage 1 – Receive the Client Request**



**## Objective**



**Capture the client request and register it in a structured way.**



**## Actions**



**\* Identify the channel where the request arrived:**



&#x20; **\* email;**

&#x20; **\* website form;**

&#x20; **\* WhatsApp;**

&#x20; **\* social media;**

&#x20; **\* partner referral.**

**\* Create a new Trello card.**

**\* Add the client name, request date, request type, and current status.**

**\* Check if the request includes urgent deadlines or travel dates.**



**## AI Support**



**AI can help summarize the original client message and identify the main request.**



**## Human Validation**



**A team member must confirm that the AI summary is accurate before using it.**



**---**



**# Stage 2 – Analyze and Summarize the Request**



**## Objective**



**Understand what the client needs before preparing a response.**



**## Actions**



**\* Read the full client message.**

**\* Identify the purpose of the request.**

**\* Identify missing information.**

**\* Separate confirmed information from assumptions.**

**\* Write a short internal summary.**



**## AI Support**



**AI can help transform a long or unclear message into a short structured summary.**



**## Human Validation**



**The team must review the summary and make sure the AI did not invent or misinterpret information.**



**---**



**# Stage 3 – Collect Missing Information**



**## Objective**



**Reduce back-and-forth communication by asking for the right information early.**



**## Actions**



**Check if the client provided:**



**\* expected travel dates;**

**\* number of people;**

**\* island or location of interest;**

**\* type of experience requested;**

**\* preferred language;**

**\* approximate budget;**

**\* special needs or restrictions.**



**If information is missing, send a short professional message requesting the missing details.**



**## AI Support**



**AI can draft a clear message asking for missing information.**



**## Human Validation**



**A team member must review the message before sending it to the client.**



**---**



**# Stage 4 – Organize the Request Internally**



**## Objective**



**Make sure the team knows who is responsible and what needs to happen next.**



**## Actions**



**\* Update the Trello card.**

**\* Set priority.**

**\* Assign a responsible team member.**

**\* Add internal notes.**

**\* Share key updates in Slack.**

**\* Create or update a Google Docs file if the request requires more documentation.**



**## AI Support**



**AI can convert rough notes into a cleaner internal summary.**



**## Human Validation**



**The team must confirm that internal notes are accurate and complete.**



**---**



**# Stage 5 – Prepare the Initial Response or Proposal**



**## Objective**



**Create a clear and useful response for the client.**



**## Actions**



**\* Check availability of activities, guides, or partners.**

**\* Confirm dates, locations, budget, and service limitations.**

**\* Prepare a simple proposal or response.**

**\* Include clear next steps.**

**\* Avoid promises that have not been confirmed internally.**



**## AI Support**



**AI can help structure the response or proposal in professional language.**



**## Human Validation**



**A team member must confirm all practical details before sending anything to the client.**



**---**



**# Stage 6 – Send the Response to the Client**



**## Objective**



**Respond to the client in a professional and consistent way.**



**## Actions**



**\* Use a clear, polite, and professional tone.**

**\* Include the information requested by the client.**

**\* Include next steps.**

**\* Keep the message simple and easy to understand.**

**\* Register the response in the Trello card.**



**## AI Support**



**AI can review the message for clarity, structure, and tone.**



**## Human Validation**



**The final message must be approved by a person before it is sent.**



**---**



**# Stage 7 – Follow Up**



**## Objective**



**Avoid losing client requests and keep communication active.**



**## Actions**



**\* Track whether the client responded.**

**\* Update the Trello status.**

**\* Send a follow-up if there is no response within the expected period.**

**\* Keep the follow-up short, polite, and relevant.**



**## AI Support**



**AI can suggest a short follow-up message.**



**## Human Validation**



**The team must confirm that the follow-up is appropriate for the client’s situation.**



**---**



**# Stage 8 – Close and Document the Request**



**## Objective**



**Close the request properly and keep useful information for future improvement.**



**## Actions**



**\* Mark the request as completed, cancelled, or pending.**

**\* Update the final Trello status.**

**\* Save useful notes.**

**\* Identify what worked well.**

**\* Identify what can be improved.**

**\* Avoid storing unnecessary sensitive information.**



**## AI Support**



**AI can help create a short closing summary.**



**## Human Validation**



**The team must confirm that no private or sensitive client information is exposed unnecessarily.**



**---**



**# Workflow Tools**



**| Tool             | Purpose                                                  |**

**| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |**

**| Trello           | Task tracking and request status                         |**

**| Slack            | Internal communication                                   |**

**| Google Docs      | Documentation and shared notes                           |**

**| Google Workspace | File organization and collaboration                      |**

**| Google Meet      | Client or team meetings                                  |**

**| AI tools         | Drafting, summarizing, improving, and organizing outputs |**



**---**



**# Quality Control Checklist**



**Before sending any AI-assisted output to a client, check:**



**\* Is the information accurate?**

**\* Is the tone professional?**

**\* Is the message clear?**

**\* Did the AI invent any details?**

**\* Are prices, dates, and availability confirmed?**

**\* Is any sensitive information exposed?**

**\* Does the output match the client’s request?**

**\* Is human approval complete?**



**---**



**# Final Workflow Summary**



**The workflow uses AI to reduce repetitive work and improve communication, but every important step includes human validation.**



**AI supports:**



**\* summarizing;**

**\* drafting;**

**\* organizing;**

**\* improving tone;**

**\* creating templates;**

**\* documenting cases.**



**The professional remains responsible for:**



**\* understanding the client;**

**\* validating information;**

**\* confirming availability;**

**\* protecting data;**

**\* making final decisions;**

**\* maintaining service quality.**



**This makes the workflow more efficient without losing accuracy, responsibility, or professionalism.**
