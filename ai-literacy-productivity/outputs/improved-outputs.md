**# Improved Outputs – AI Literacy for Productivity**



**## Purpose**



**This file contains the improved versions of the professional outputs created during the AI Productivity Sprint.**



**The initial outputs were reviewed and then improved using more structured prompts. The improved prompts included more context, clearer objectives, professional tone, format requirements, and validation criteria.**



**---**



**# Output 1 – Improved Professional Client Response Email**



**## Improved Prompt Used**



**```text**

**Assume the role of a communication assistant for Cabo Digital Travel, a tourism and local experiences company in Cape Verde.**



**Create a professional email to respond to an international client interested in local experiences and customized tours.**



**The email should be cordial, clear, and objective. It should thank the client for contacting the company, briefly explain the service, ask for important information needed to prepare a proposal, and suggest either an online meeting or continuing the conversation by email.**



**Do not promise availability before confirmation.**



**Write in simple professional English.**

**```**



**## Improved Output**



**\*\*Subject: Thank You for Your Interest in Cabo Digital Travel\*\***



**Hello,**



**Thank you for contacting \*\*Cabo Digital Travel\*\*.**



**We would be happy to help you plan a personalized experience in Cape Verde based on your travel dates, interests, and preferred type of activity. Our services include local experiences, customized tours, and support in organizing activities for national and international visitors.**



**To prepare a more accurate proposal, could you please share the following information?**



**\* Expected travel dates**

**\* Number of people**

**\* Island or location of interest**

**\* Type of experience you are looking for**

**\* Preferred language**

**\* Approximate budget, if already defined**

**\* Any special needs or preferences**



**Once we receive this information, we can review the available options and send you an initial proposal. If you prefer, we can also schedule a short online meeting to better understand your needs.**



**We remain available to support you in planning your experience.**



**Best regards,**

**Cabo Digital Travel Team**



**## Improvements Made**



**The improved version is more useful and professional because it:**



**\* thanks the client clearly;**

**\* explains the company’s service in simple language;**

**\* asks for specific information needed to prepare a proposal;**

**\* reduces unnecessary back-and-forth communication;**

**\* includes a clear next step;**

**\* offers the option of an online meeting;**

**\* avoids promising availability before internal confirmation;**

**\* uses a professional but friendly tone.**



**## Validation Notes**



**Before sending this email to a real client, the team should validate:**



**\* service availability;**

**\* correct prices;**

**\* accurate tour options;**

**\* language preferences;**

**\* any special client requirements;**

**\* whether an online meeting is necessary;**

**\* if the tone matches the company’s communication style.**

**# Output 2 – Improved Operational Checklist for Client Requests**



**## Improved Prompt Used**



**```text**

**Assume the role of a digital operations consultant specialized in productivity and remote work.**



**Create a simple operational checklist for Cabo Digital Travel to manage client requests in a more organized way.**



**The company is a small tourism and local experiences business that works partially remotely and uses Google Workspace, Trello, Slack, Google Meet, and generative AI tools.**



**The checklist should cover the process from receiving a client request to final follow-up.**



**Include where AI can support the process and where human validation is required.**



**Use clear, practical, and professional English.**

**```**



**## Improved Output**



**# Operational Checklist – Client Request Management**



**\*\*Cabo Digital Travel\*\***



**## 1. Receive the Client Request**



**\* Identify the request channel: email, website form, WhatsApp, social media, or partner referral.**

**\* Register the request in Trello.**

**\* Create a card with the client name, request date, request type, and current status.**

**\* Check if the request includes urgent dates or deadlines.**



**\*\*AI support:\*\* summarize the client’s message and identify the main request.**

**\*\*Human validation:\*\* confirm that the AI summary is accurate before using it.**



**---**



**## 2. Collect Essential Information**



**Check if the client provided:**



**\* travel dates;**

**\* number of people;**

**\* island or location of interest;**

**\* type of experience requested;**

**\* preferred language;**

**\* approximate budget;**

**\* special needs or restrictions.**



**If information is missing, send a short professional message requesting the missing details.**



**\*\*AI support:\*\* draft a clear message asking for missing information.**

**\*\*Human validation:\*\* review the tone, facts, and client context before sending.**



**---**



**## 3. Organize the Request Internally**



**\* Update the Trello card.**

**\* Set the request priority.**

**\* Assign a responsible team member.**

**\* Share relevant updates in Slack.**

**\* Create or update a Google Docs file if more detailed documentation is needed.**



**\*\*AI support:\*\* turn rough notes into a structured summary.**

**\*\*Human validation:\*\* confirm that the request details are complete and correct.**



**---**



**## 4. Prepare the Initial Proposal**



**\* Check availability of experiences, guides, or partners.**

**\* Confirm date, location, budget, and service limitations.**

**\* Prepare a proposal with clear options.**

**\* Include the next steps for the client.**



**\*\*AI support:\*\* help structure the proposal in professional language.**

**\*\*Human validation:\*\* confirm availability, prices, conditions, and feasibility before sending.**



**---**



**## 5. Send the Client Response**



**\* Use a professional, friendly, and clear tone.**

**\* Respond within the internal response-time target.**

**\* Include the main information and next steps.**

**\* Avoid promises that have not been confirmed internally.**



**\*\*AI support:\*\* review clarity, structure, and tone.**

**\*\*Human validation:\*\* approve the final message before sending.**



**---**



**## 6. Follow Up**



**\* Track the client’s response.**

**\* Update the Trello card status.**

**\* Send an internal reminder if there is no response within the expected time.**

**\* Follow up with the client using a short and polite message.**



**\*\*AI support:\*\* suggest a follow-up message.**

**\*\*Human validation:\*\* confirm that the follow-up is appropriate for the situation.**



**---**



**## 7. Close and Document the Request**



**\* Mark the request as completed, cancelled, or pending.**

**\* Update the final status in Trello.**

**\* Save useful notes for future reference.**

**\* Identify what could be improved in the process.**



**\*\*AI support:\*\* summarize the case and create a short closing note.**

**\*\*Human validation:\*\* make sure no sensitive client data is exposed unnecessarily.**



**---**



**## Improvements Made**



**The improved checklist is more useful than the initial version because it:**



**\* includes the company’s remote-work tools;**

**\* creates a clearer operational process;**

**\* includes AI support points;**

**\* includes human validation points;**

**\* improves task organization;**

**\* supports documentation;**

**\* helps standardize communication;**

**\* reduces repetitive administrative work;**

**\* gives the team a more practical workflow to follow.**



**## Validation Notes**



**Before using this checklist in a real company, the team should define:**



**\* who is responsible for each step;**

**\* expected response times;**

**\* where client information should be stored;**

**\* which data should not be entered into AI tools;**

**\* approval rules before proposals are sent;**

**\* how Trello statuses should be named;**

**\* how completed requests should be archived.**

**The initial prompts produced basic results. The improved prompts created more practical and professional outputs because they included:**

**\* business context;**

**\* audience;**

**\* expected format;**

**\* operational tools;**

**\* tone;**
