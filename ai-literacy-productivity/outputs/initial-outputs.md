**# Initial Outputs – AI Literacy for Productivity**



**## Purpose**



**This file contains the first AI-generated outputs created during the exercise.**



**The goal of saving the initial versions is to show the difference between a basic AI response and a more professional version improved through iterative prompting.**



**These outputs were created before adding more context, structure, constraints, and validation criteria.**



**---**



**# Output 1 – Professional Client Response Email**



**## Initial Prompt Used**



**```text**

**Create an email to respond to a client interested in Cabo Digital Travel's services.**

**```**



**## Initial Output**



**\*\*Subject: Thank You for Your Interest in Cabo Digital Travel\*\***



**Hello,**



**Thank you for contacting us and for your interest in Cabo Digital Travel.**



**We would be happy to help you organize your experience in Cape Verde. Our company works with local experiences and customized tours, and we can provide more information about our services and availability.**



**Please let us know what type of experience you are interested in, and we will be glad to assist you.**



**Best regards,**

**Cabo Digital Travel**



**## Initial Review**



**This first version is polite and clear, but it is too generic.**



**It does not collect enough information from the client, such as travel dates, number of people, island or location, type of experience, preferred language, or approximate budget.**



**It also does not help the company reduce back-and-forth communication, because the client would probably need to be contacted again for missing details.**



**## Limitations Identified**



**\* The message is too general.**

**\* It does not ask enough diagnostic or planning questions.**

**\* It does not support an efficient workflow.**

**\* It does not show enough personalization.**

**\* It does not include a clear next step.**

**\* It could be stronger for an international client.**

**\* It does not mention an online meeting option.**

**\* It does not include a caution about confirming availability before promising services.**

**# Output 2 – Operational Checklist for Client Requests**



**## Initial Prompt Used**



**```text**

**Create a checklist for Cabo Digital Travel to organize client requests.**

**```**



**## Initial Output**



**# Basic Client Request Checklist**



**1. Receive the client request.**

**2. Confirm the client’s main information.**

**3. Check service availability.**

**4. Prepare a response.**

**5. Send the response to the client.**

**6. Organize the booking details.**

**7. Confirm payment or next steps.**

**8. Follow up with the client.**

**9. Close the request after completion.**



**## Initial Review**



**This checklist is useful as a very basic structure, but it is not detailed enough for a real remote-work operation.**



**It does not mention the tools used by the company, such as Trello, Slack, Google Workspace, Google Meet, or AI tools. It also does not explain who should validate information, how tasks should be documented, or how AI can support repetitive work.**



**## Limitations Identified**



**\* The checklist is too simple.**

**\* It does not include remote-work tools.**

**\* It does not show where AI can support the workflow.**

**\* It does not identify where human validation is required.**

**\* It does not include task ownership or priority.**

**\* It does not support documentation.**

**\* It does not help standardize communication.**

**\* It does not provide enough detail for a small remote team.**



**---**



**# General Observation**



**The initial outputs were useful as starting points, but they were not strong enough for professional use.**



**They needed more context, clearer structure, better alignment with the company’s operational challenges, and stronger validation steps.**



**This confirmed that the first AI output should not be accepted automatically. It should be reviewed, improved, and adapted to the real context before being used professionally.**
